Percentage
Simple fraction and their percentages

$$\frac{1}{2}=50$$

$$\frac{1}{4}=25$$

$$\frac{1}{5}=20$$

Percentage Price Increase/Decrease

If a price of a article is increased from Rs 'a' to Rs 'b' then to keep the expenditure same, the percentage decrease in the expenses will be calculated as:

$$(\frac{b-a}{b}\times100)$$

Calculating Total Income

If a person spends 'x%' of income on food, 'y%' of remaining on rent and 'z%' of remaining on clothes. If he has Rs P remaining, then total income of the person will be calculated as:

$$\frac{p\times100\times100\times100}{(100-x)(100-y)(100-z)}$$
